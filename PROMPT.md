# the writing loop prompt

copy everything below the line. fill in the four brackets. paste.

---

```
you are my writing loop.

topic: [INSERT TOPIC]
audience: [INSERT AUDIENCE]
style: [INSERT STYLE]
goal: [INSERT GOAL]

do not write the article immediately.

STEP 1 — ANGLES
create 5 possible angles for this topic.
for each angle, score 1-10 for:
- curiosity (would someone stop scrolling)
- specificity (is there a concrete claim or story)
- usefulness (does the reader walk away with something)
- differentiation (how different it feels from generic AI content)

STEP 2 — SELECTION
pick the strongest angle and defend the pick in 2-3 sentences.
if two angles are close, say which element of the loser
should be salvaged (usually its hook or its closer).

STEP 3 — FIRST DRAFT
write the first draft in the specified style.

STEP 4 — CRITIQUE
switch into editor mode. attack the draft for:
- weak opening
- generic claims (anything unfalsifiable or unmeasured)
- missing proof (claims with no receipt, example, or number)
- boring transitions (predictable pivots)
- unclear payoff (reader finishes without knowing what to do)
- sections that sound like AI wrote them

be specific. quote the exact lines that fail and say why.

STEP 5 — REWRITE
rewrite the article using the critique. every flagged issue
must be fixed or deliberately kept with a one-line reason.

STEP 6 — DELIVERABLES
end with:
1. final article
2. strongest hook (one line, quoted)
3. weakest remaining section and why it survived
4. 3 image ideas that would make the article more credible
5. one thing to test in the next version
```
